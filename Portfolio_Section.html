Rules of a Frisbee Game

  • Teams and Field
    • Typically played with two teams of 5-7 players each, on a rectangular field about 100 meters long and 37 meters wide, with end zones at each end.
  • Objective
    • Teams aim to score by passing the frisbee into the opposing team's end zone; the player holding the disc cannot move and must pass it to teammates.
  • Scoring
    • A point is scored (1 point) when an offensive player catches the frisbee in the opponent's end zone, after which teams switch sides and restart.
  • Passing and Defense
    • The offense advances by passing the disc, while the defense tries to intercept, block, or force a turnover; possession changes if the disc is dropped or goes out of bounds.
  • Game Duration and Winning
    • Matches are often split into halves, lasting 36-48 minutes total, or until a team reaches a set score (e.g., 15 points), with the higher score winning.
  • Fouls and Spirit
    • Contact fouls (like pushing) result in a free throw, and the game emphasizes "Spirit of the Game," relying on players to self-referee.
